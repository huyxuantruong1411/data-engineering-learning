Chihayafuru addpermalink
Chihayafuru and Kono Oto Tomare! each involve a club for a niche Japanese competitive activity/sport. There is a similar feel to the storytelling revolving around the clubs for karuta (traditional Japanese card game) and the koto (traditional Japanese stringed instrument). The clubs in both have male and female members, with friendship and teamwork playing a big part as they strive together to improve and hope to reach the nationals. In Chihayafuru, Chihaya decides to become the Queen of karuta (top female karuta player), after witnessing her friend's passion for the game. Similarly in Kono Oto Tomare!, Chika finds out he wants to pursue understanding what's so great about the koto, after first seeing the passion his grandfather had for it. The art in each is great, and it helps to accentuate the emotions of the characters. As one grows closer to the characters, the art helps relay the feelings of pain in their struggles, and joys in their accomplishments to the reader. I'd recommend both Chihayafuru and Kono Oto Tomare! to anyone who can find any of this appealing to them, as there is much more to enjoy after reading it for yourself. read more

If you like Chihayafuru I think you will like Kono Oto Tomare!. If you are interested learning about traditional Japanese culture in manga, these mangas feature a club dedicated to that. In Chihayafuru, we have a girl starting a club dedicated to a traditional Japanese game- karuta - while in Kono Oto Tomare! we have a boy continuing a club in danger of disbanding for the playing of a traditional Japanese musical instrument - the koto. They have to go through trials to run clubs that are not popular and don't have a lot of practicioners, and each has a member who is at a mastery level and others who are beginners. I guess the biggest similarity is the main characters love and dedication to what they do. There are boy and girl members in the clubs too and they are mangas that will appeal to male and female readers alike; although Chihayafuru is technically a josei and Kono Oto Tomare is a shounen, I enjoy them both so don't let labels put you off. They are written in such a way that there are things to appeal to both. They both also have appealing art. The characters in Kono Oto Tomare! have personality conflicts that spice it up too, the male lead is a tsundere/ delinquent. I recommend both of them! read more
