Synopsis

The consultation room of Yonggang University's psychology department is run by the genius Dr. Frost. Skilled at reading people, he is an easily recognizable figure with his shocking white hair and stoic face. But Dr. Frost has one fatal flaw—he lacks certain emotions, like love and empathy. Despite this, he solves every case that comes his way using his expansive textbook knowledge and sharp perception.

Aiding him in his work is his assistant Yoon Seong-Ah, a young psychology student at the university. As the two work together to uncover the emotions and motivations of others, Dr. Frost begins to discover a part of himself which he lost long ago.

[Written by MAL Rewrite]

Edit
Background

Dr. Frost is originally a webtoon series which has been officially published in book format by Anibooks (애니북스) since May 18, 2012. It was adapted into a live-action TV series from November 23, 2014, to February 1, 2015.

The series was published digitally in English by Webtoon Entertainment from December 27, 2014, to August 4, 2023.

Recommended
Dr. Frost consist of two parts.

First, it's about a psychologist finding out the problems in people and trying to fix them. These parts are really good. The writer worked with real professionals from the field so it's on the realistic side. As you can see from the other preliminary reviews, reading it is recommended.

Second, with a classic webtoon twist the story drifts away from the original synopsis and takes on a criminal turn. I wasn't fond of these detective plots. There were also some parts about Dr. Frost's character progression but at that point you might just go watch Dr. House. I've waited for years ... for the second part but it was just a waste of time and patience. I don't recommend reading beyond the parts where Dr. Frost keeps a grip of himself at one point.

Karinara
Recommended
Preliminary (121/258 eps)
This review is straight from me finishing the first 121 chapters of this manhwa that have been translated to English.

Story:
The story is a collection of cases of psychological consultation. All the cases were different with different outcomes and it can be very well seen that the author of this manhwa, Lee Jong-bum knows what he is doing. Also the story between cases about the main characters is very well written and interesting.
As someone, who has studied psychology (just basics in high school but still) I really loved to see familiar theories and experiments being part of the cases.

Art:
The art style is very similar to other ... webtoon manhwas I've seen, but I think this style is very beautiful and fits the manhwa very well. The characters are beautiful and the backgrounds are great and realistic looking.

Characters:
Both the main characters and characters of each case are unique and have their own unique personalities and back stories. I am of course very interested in the main character, Frost, his personality and his backstory, which all of course are a big part of this manhwa.

Enjoyment:
I read all of the available chapters in just few hours, because I just couldn't stop reading and I was a bit sad when it ended (for now) so I would say I got a lot of enjoyment out of this.

Overall: I honestly have nothing bad to say about this.
