It's written by the same author: Watashi ni xx Shinasai is about a girl who has been cut from social contact from her peers, resulting in the fact that she knows no social skills. Although, Watashi Ni xx Shinasai is more bold, whereas Koko ni Iru Yo is a cute manga. "Koko ni Iru Yo" is also about a loner girl, though it's because she has no presence. Watashitachi ni wa Kabe ga Aru. addpermalink
1. The ability to hook. 2. Character development 3. Love (Pure love) 4. Childhood friend love, however that is not the main protagonists love interest. 5. Enjoyment (of course!) Looking back, and directly at the two mangas, it's apparent that they're nothing alike. However, both are great to follow and allow you to watch your main protagonist learn about love, and about relationships. You get to see their personal struggles, and develop over time. Watashitachi ni wa Kabe ga Aru, features a young girl who confuses what love is. She manipulates this cold boy, to help her experience love for her phone novel series. There's a love triangle, that eventually is sorted out. Luckily this manga isn't about their love triangle, but is about the overall development of the protagonist and continues farther than the triangle. Each character has something to overcome. This isn't a typical shoujo, though at first glance you might think that. There comes a point though, once you sit in for the ride even more, those little nicks and knacks you see in the female protagonists ideas, are corrected by the author. What is similar is that this is indeed a love story, but unlike Watashitachi ni wa Kabe, Watashi ni xx Shinasai focuses on a few characters internal struggles (while still giving the protagonist her light.) They're both enjoyable reads. It's also farther into the series, and requires a bit of dedication to get the upmost enjoyment out of this, because the protagonist spends a great deal of time figuring out what love is.  read more

Hatsukoi Wazurai addpermalink
The moment, I read Hatsukoi Wazurai, Watashi ni xx Shinasai popped into my head. Both heroines learn about love. While Yukina learns it for the sake of her stories, Hotaru genuinely wants to learn in order to "change." In both cases, the heroines learn through experience with a popular student. Eventually, the heroes of these tales both fall in love with the heroines. There's a huge significance to the pacing of the story. Watashi ni xx Shinasai can be dragging with how it's carried out, but each chapter provides at least one deep, meaningful lesson (plus the drawing is amazing!) and I guarantee that it will leave you wanting for more. Whereas Hatsukoi Wazurai is up-beat the moment you read the story. In just the first chapter, you can already see a relationship blooming between the protagonists. Watashi ni xx Shinasai takes a dark turn after reading the first 10 chapters of the story. It gets pretty intense, once you're engrossed with the story. All the more, it makes the story interesting and unique. It's one of the best mangas to portray the meaning of "love" in multiple aspects. So if you enjoyed reading Hatsukoi Wazurai, then you will definitely love Watashi ni xx Shinasai. read more
