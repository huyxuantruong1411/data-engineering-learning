If you’re a fan of Berserk and looking for something new to dive into, Kubera might just be what you need. While the two stories are quite different in tone and style, they share a love for complex narratives and deep, thought-provoking themes that keep you hooked. Berserk is renowned for its dark and gritty exploration of human suffering, fate, and the eternal battle between good and evil. It doesn’t shy away from showing the harsh realities of its world, where every victory feels hard-earned and often comes at a high cost. Kubera, on the other hand, offers a more fantastical journey but with its own brand of intensity. It’s set in a universe filled with gods, magic, and multiple realms, and it digs deep into themes like destiny, power, and the nature of existence. While it might not be as graphically violent, Kubera balances its darker moments with humor and philosophical insights, making for a rich and layered experience. Both stories excel in world-building. Berserk pulls you into its medieval nightmare, where every corner hides a new horror. Meanwhile, Kubera gives you an expansive, multi-layered universe that’s brimming with mythological intrigue and a complex system of powers. The characters in Kubera, much like in Berserk, are deeply developed and often shaped by their struggles. Whether it’s the relentless Guts or the determined Kubera Leez, both series give you protagonists you can root for as they face overwhelming odds. And let’s not forget the art. Berserk is famous for its stunning, detailed, and often brutal artwork. Kubera might start off simpler, but it evolves beautifully over time, capturing the epic scope and emotional depth of the story. So, if you’re into Berserk’s intense storytelling and richly developed world, Kubera offers a different but equally compelling adventure that’s well worth your time. read more
